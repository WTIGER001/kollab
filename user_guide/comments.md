# User Guide: Document Comments & Nested Threads

Collaborative comments allow team members to discuss document content, ask questions, leave feedback, and thread discussions directly at the bottom of any page.

---

## 1. Viewing Comments

At the bottom of every active wiki document page, you will see a **Comments** section:
- It shows the total count of comments posted (e.g. `2 Comments`).
- Top-level comments are displayed chronologically (oldest on top, newest at the bottom).
- Nested replies are indented directly beneath their parent comment.
- Each comment is labeled with the author's color-coded **User Initials Avatar** (which extracts the first letter of each word in their display name up to 3 initials).

---

## 2. Posting Comments & Replies

### Posting a New Comment
1. Scroll to the bottom of the page.
2. Under the comments list, click the input box displaying `Write a comment...`.
3. Type your comment and click **Post Comment** to publish.

### Replying to an Existing Comment
1. Locate the comment you want to reply to.
2. Click the small **Reply** action link underneath that comment.
3. An inline text box will open. Type your reply and click **Reply** to nest it under that thread.

---

## 3. Liking Comments

You can react to helpful comments or express agreement using the **Like** action:
- Click the **Like** button under any comment or reply.
- The button will highlight, and the count of likes will increment.
- Clicking it again will remove your like and decrement the counter.

---

## 4. Editing and Deleting Your Comments

You can only edit or delete comments that you personally created.

### Editing a Comment
1. Click the **Edit** action link under your comment.
2. The comment text will be replaced by an inline text field containing the current content.
3. Modify the content as desired and click **Save**. Click **Cancel** to discard changes.

### Deleting a Comment
1. Click the **Delete** action link under your comment.
2. Confirm the deletion popup dialog.
3. The comment (and any associated replies) will be removed.

---

## ⚠️ Comment Restrictions

- **Read-Only / Deleted Pages**: If a page is inside the Trash Bin, the comments section is set to read-only. You can read existing comments, but posting new comments, replies, edits, and deletions is disabled.
